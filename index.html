NeeroNet

What is NeeroNet?

NeeroNet is an integrated and intelligent real­time water assessment model with smart analytics. Using machine learning and IoT to efficiently monitor water usage, NeeroNet can survey different activities of industry, household and agriculture in urban and rural regions of developing countries. In addition, our system will monitor rainwater harvested for groundwater replenishment when installed in channelized tanks on rooftops and alert for leakages in the distribution network when installed in the pipelines.

Our Mission

Our mission is to manage the water crisis of the world, and specifically of developing nations like India and China, by introducing IoT and Big Data analytics enabled smart water management for better water distribution, assessment and management. We believe that our proposed business model can be applied in many different markets. NeeroNet’s vision is to manage the water channels and network in the state of Delhi in India and meet the water­related technological gaps of the region. NeeroNet has the potential to reduce water wastage, improve water treatment and disposal at the micro level, and monitor the scarce water supplies in the region.

The Problem

Water is a critical commodity and limited resource in developing countries such as India. Developing countries often have i​nefficient water distribution, assessment and management models​. Specifically, India faces an immense water shortage due to improper management of water by the government and natural disasters. In New Delhi​, it is estimated that nearly 4​0% of water is being lost to leaking pipes.​A study conducted by the Delhi Committee of the Associated Chambers of Commerce and Industry of India (ASSOCHAM) reported that water distribution losses are due to leakages in a network of 9​,000 km­long water supply lines. Approximately 3000 million liters are pumped by government water centers but only about 1700 million liters actually reach consumers. In addition, many households go without water for extended periods of time. Studies show that 2​7% of homes in Delhi receive tap water for less than three hours a day. The current gap between supply and demand of water is nearly 1​,300 million liters of water. There is a demand of 4,300 million liters per day but the Delhi government supplies only about 3,000 million liters of water to its residents per day. An analytical analysis of government data led to the following conclusions. In terms of residential water consumption, residential communities with a population of between 20,000 to 100,000 require 100 to 150 liters per person per day. In addition, there is a wide disparity in water consumption in India among different socioeconomic levels. High income households tend to consume 250 liters per person per day. However, lower­income households live with as little as 40 liters per person per day. Moreover, although current methods can analyze the physical, chemical and biological parameter of water, it has several drawbacks, including poor spatiotemporal coverage labor intensive processes, high cost for labor, operation and equipment, and the lack of real time water quality information to enable critical decisions for public health protection.

Proposed Solution

We hope to remove inefficiencies in the water transmission, distribution, assessment and management processes in India. Our technology will allow different stakeholders or communities to easily monitor water consumption. Our simple metering and wastage statistical tools will allow users to forecast water usage and wastage. NeeroNet leverages the recent advancements in IoT and Big data analytics to address the complexity of the water industry and propose site­specific solutions within the broader context of integrated water management approaches.

Primary Components

The purpose of NeeroNet is to develop a low cost system comprising of modules for real time monitoring of the water consumption analysis in an IoT environment and a Business Intelligence enabled Dashboard. The design system applies a specialized IoT module for accessing sensor data on mobile phones and computers. The primary components of our system includes sensors (installed in pipes, water storage units, water outlet channels), BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy installed per room to receive signal from the sensors which then sends the readings to the smart meter via BLE or WiFi), Raspberry Pi based smart meter which collate data from BLE modules and sends the overall home data to SmartThings Open cloud to analyze data in real­time via WiFi or SIGFOX.

Impact

We hope to replace existing conventional water meters with NeeroNet Kit across different cities in India, which will allow for proper distribution of water by mapping water usage from homes to states of the country, water conservation by timely alerts for leakages, and preparedness for natural disasters.

Magnitude of Impact

NeeroNet can impact the millions of people in India who currently have limited access to water. We hope to begin by targeting government agencies in Delhi, who have the highest stake in water. In later stages, we will specifically develop systems to apply our technology to various industries, residential homes and agriculture across India. We believe our smart analytics model can drastically reduce the water consumption by removing the inefficiencies in the current water distribution system.
